Chile Relleno de Puebla

Description

Chile Relleno de Puebla is a beloved Mexican dish that combines smoky roasted poblano peppers with a rich, cheesy filling, all wrapped in a fluffy egg batter and lightly fried to golden perfection. It's comforting, satisfying, and surprisingly easy to prepare at home.

This dish brings together bold flavors — savory, slightly spicy, and wonderfully cheesy — all balanced by a simple tomato-based sauce that adds depth and brightness. Whether served as a main course or part of a festive meal, these stuffed peppers are a true crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

  • 3 large poblano chiles
  • 1 1/2 cups Oaxaca, Monterey Jack, or mozzarella cheese (shredded)
  • 3 eggs, separated
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • Salt to taste
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • For the sauce:
  • 3 Roma tomatoes
  • 1/4 white onion
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Steps

  1. Roast the chiles: Place poblano chiles over an open flame or under a broiler until the skin is blackened and blistered. Transfer to a covered bowl or sealed plastic bag to steam for 10 minutes, then peel off the skin. Carefully make a slit and remove seeds.
  2. Stuff the chiles: Fill each chile with shredded cheese, then gently close the opening with toothpicks if needed.
  3. Prepare the batter: Beat egg whites until stiff peaks form, then gently fold in the yolks and a pinch of salt. Lightly dust each stuffed chile with flour, then coat with the egg batter.
  4. Fry the chiles: Heat oil in a skillet over medium heat. Fry the battered chiles until golden brown on all sides. Drain on paper towels.
  5. Make the sauce: Blend tomatoes, onion, garlic, broth, salt, and pepper until smooth. Simmer in a saucepan for 10–15 minutes until slightly thickened.
  6. Serve: Pour warm tomato sauce over the chiles and serve immediately. Pair with rice or beans for a full meal.
